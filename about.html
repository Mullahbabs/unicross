History & Background

The University of Cross River State (UNICROSS) was established as a beacon of academic excellence and a driver of socio-economic development within Cross River State and beyond. With its roots deeply intertwined with the state’s rich cultural heritage, the university was founded to provide accessible, high-quality education and to cultivate a generation of thinkers, innovators, and leaders.

UNICROSS began its journey with a commitment to meeting the educational and developmental needs of its community while aligning with global standards of higher learning. Over the years, it has grown to encompass a diverse range of academic programs across various disciplines, catering to the aspirations of students from all walks of life.

Known for its vibrant academic environment, experienced faculty, and state-of-the-art facilities, UNICROSS has consistently maintained its reputation as a hub for intellectual and personal growth. The university remains steadfast in its mission to foster innovation, research, and community engagement, ensuring that its graduates are equipped to excel in a rapidly evolving world.

The Journey So Far

The transformation from the Cross River University of Technology (CRUTECH) to the University of Cross River State (UNICROSS) represents a significant milestone in the institution's history. Established initially as CRUTECH, the university focused on technology-driven programs, offering specialized courses that addressed the technological and vocational needs of Cross River State and its environs.
As the educational landscape evolved and the demand for a broader spectrum of academic disciplines grew, the institution recognized the need to expand its scope. This strategic decision led to the rebranding and restructuring of CRUTECH into UNICROSS, a conventional university committed to offering a comprehensive range of programs across diverse fields of study.
The transition marked a turning point in the university’s mission and vision. While CRUTECH was known for its emphasis on science, technology, and technical education, UNICROSS now offers programs in arts, humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, and professional disciplines. This expansion has made the university more inclusive, providing opportunities for students from various academic interests and backgrounds. The change of name and focus underscores the institution's commitment to adaptability, growth, and meeting the ever-changing needs of society. By embracing this broader academic identity, UNICROSS has reinforced its role as a hub for innovation, research, and community development, paving the way for a brighter future for its students and the region it serves.
